Labels and Definitions - Classification Task

Labels and Definitions:

1. IGO: A formal organization whose members are countries or states, with three or more members. Most definitions in the literature also require a permanent secretariat or other indication of institutionalization, such as headquarters, staff, and budgets.

2. IGO-FUND: A subset of IGOs, serving as financial instruments, typically consisting of pooled contributions from states, and managed by a specific IGO.

3. Bilateral donor or actor: A government or state organization not from {country} that is funding and/or carrying out the provision of goods and services outside of its territory.

4. GOV: The government of {country} where goods and services are provided, either by a domestic actor or an external actor like the UN.

5. INGO: A nongovernmental, not-for-profit organization active in more than one country, with advocacy and/or service delivery as its primary purpose, not established by an intergovernmental agreement.

6. NNGO: A nongovernmental, not-for-profit organization active in only one country, focusing on advocacy and/or service delivery for the broader society rather than just its membership.

7. International civil society: A nongovernmental, not-for-profit organization active in more than one country, with purposes other than advocacy or service delivery, such as religious or academic interests.

8. National civil society: A nongovernmental, not-for-profit organization active in only one country, with a primary focus on specific interests rather than the general public.

9. International company: A for-profit organization active in more than one country, including multinational enterprises.

10. National company: A for-profit organization active in only one country.
